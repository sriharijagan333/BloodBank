Why Donate

The gift you give
is irreplaceable

Blood cannot be made in a laboratory. It cannot be manufactured, synthesised, or created artificially. It can only come from one source — a generous human being like you.

"The blood you donate today could be flowing through a child's veins tomorrow, giving them the chance to grow up."

Your donation is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — each component helping a different patient. One act of generosity, three lives touched.

Eligibility

Are you ready to
give life?

  • ✓
    Age 18–65 — healthy adults in this range are eligible to donate whole blood
  • ✓
    Weight ≥ 50 kg — minimum weight ensures donor safety during collection
  • ✓
    Haemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL — checked on site with a quick finger-prick test
  • ✓
    Good general health — feeling well, no active infection or fever on donation day
  • ✓
    56+ days since last donation — allows your body to fully replenish
  • ✗
    Currently on antibiotics — wait until 7 days after completing the course
  • ✗
    Tattoo or piercing in last 6 months — temporary deferral for safety screening
  • ✗
    Travel to malaria-risk areas recently — wait period applies; ask our team

Questions

Everything you need
to know

Does donating blood hurt? +
Most donors feel only a brief sting from the initial needle insertion — similar to a mosquito bite. The donation itself is painless. Our trained staff ensure your comfort throughout. Over 90% of donors say the experience was easier than they expected.
How long does the process take? +
The entire visit takes about 45–60 minutes: registration (5 min), mini-physical (10 min), donation (8–10 min), and rest with refreshments (15 min). The actual blood collection is only about 8–10 minutes.
How often can I donate blood? +
Whole blood donors must wait at least 56 days (8 weeks) between donations. This means you can donate up to 6 times per year. Plasma donors can donate more frequently — up to every 28 days.
Will I feel weak after donating? +
Most people feel completely normal within a few hours. We recommend drinking extra fluids and eating well for 24 hours. Avoid strenuous exercise on the day of donation. Your body replaces the fluid component within 24 hours and red blood cells within 4–6 weeks.
Is the equipment sterile and safe? +
Absolutely. Every needle and collection bag is brand new, sterile, and used only once. You cannot contract any infection from donating blood. Our equipment meets all WHO and national blood safety standards.
Can I donate if I take medication? +
It depends on the medication. Common medications like blood pressure pills and cholesterol medication don't prevent donation. Antibiotics require a 7-day wait after finishing the course. Blood thinners and some other medications may defer you. Always inform our staff of your medications when you arrive.
What happens to my blood after donation? +
Your blood is tested for compatibility and infectious diseases, then separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets. Each component treats different patients. Red cells last 42 days; platelets last 5 days; plasma can be frozen for up to 1 year. This is why regular donors are so vital.
Do I get any benefits for donating? +
Blood donation is voluntary. You receive a free mini health check (blood pressure, haemoglobin, blood group confirmation), refreshments, and the deep satisfaction of knowing you've potentially saved three lives. Many regular donors also report improved wellbeing and reduced risk of certain cardiovascular conditions.
